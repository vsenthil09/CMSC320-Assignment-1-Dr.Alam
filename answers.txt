Q3: What is the difference between merging and rebasing?

Answer: The difference between merging and rebasing is that merging combines the history of the two 
branches, so it makes a new merge commit. While rebasing rewrites the commit history, so it applies 
the commit front the current branch on top of another branch. Merging keeps the history of both 
branches while rebasing create a linear history.
